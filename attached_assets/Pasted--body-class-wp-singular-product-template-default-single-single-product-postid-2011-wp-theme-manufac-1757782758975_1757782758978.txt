	<h2>Description</h2>

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>Hot Dip Galvanized Channels</strong></span></h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Hot Dip Galvanized Channels, is a <strong>Mild Steel Channels </strong>that has been hot dipped in a zinc rich galvanizing process. Galvanized Steel Channel is ideal for all general fabrication and repairs where greater strength and very good corrosion resistance is required.</p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Galvanized Channels can be used to create any construction connections, e.g. facades, precast concrete construction, residential construction and traffic engineering (e.g. tunnels), and for lift constructions, infrastructure projects such as bridges or crane runway fixings, Switchyard structures.</span></p>
<p>M/s <a href="https://www.textronsteelalloys.com/"><strong>Textron Steel and Alloys</strong></a> is Major Stockiest, Supplier, Wholesaler of Hot Dip <a href="https://www.textronsteelalloys.com/product-category/galvanized/">Galvanized</a> Channels, <a href="https://www.textronsteelalloys.com/product/galvanized-angles-supplier-stockiest/">Angles</a> for Structural Purpose.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<ul>

<li><span style="color: #000000;">&nbsp; Machining / Grinding = Good</span></li>

<li><span style="color: #000000;">&nbsp; Corrosion Resistance = Excellent</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h4><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>Advantages of Galvanized Materials :</strong></span></h4>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Lowest first cost</strong>. Galvanizing is lower in first cost than any other commonly specified protective coatings for steel.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Less maintenance/Lowest long term cost</strong>. Galvanizing is almost invariably cheapest in the long term because it lasts longer and needs less maintenance</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Long life</strong>. The life expectancy of galvanized coatings on typical structural members is far in excess of 50 years in most rural environments, and 20 to 25 years plus, even in severe urban and coastal exposure.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Reliability</strong>. Galvanizing is carried out as per International Standard 2629/2633/6745/4759 with minimum thickness of 86 microns.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Toughest coating</strong>. A galvanized coating has a unique metallurgical structure which gives outstanding resistance to mechanical damage in transport, erection and service.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Ease of inspection</strong>. Galvanized coatings are assessed readily by eye, and simple non-destructive thickness testing methods can be used.</span></p>
